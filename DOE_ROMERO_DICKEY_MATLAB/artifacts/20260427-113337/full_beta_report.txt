Romero-Dickey beta report
==========================

lambda = 5.32e-07 m
f = 0.429 m
input 1/e^2 intensity diameter = 0.005 m

paper ri = input_1e2_radius_m / sqrt(2) = 0.00176776695 m
target size x = 0.00033 m, y = 0.00012 m
Ro definition = full_size_over_sqrt_pi
Ro_x = target_size_x_m / sqrt(pi) = 0.000186182563 m
Ro_y = target_size_y_m / sqrt(pi) = 6.770275e-05 m

beta = 2*pi*ri*Ro/(lambda*f)
beta_x = 9.060975545
beta_y = 3.294900198

Interpretation:
- beta is the key dimensionless difficulty parameter in the Romero-Dickey stationary-phase approximation.
- Larger beta usually gives a more geometrical-optics-like mapping and sharper flat-top behavior.
- beta_y is smaller because the requested y size is only 120 um, so y-edge sharpness and uniformity are expected to be harder.
- The 15 mm aperture is a clear aperture mask, not the 5 mm Gaussian beam diameter.
